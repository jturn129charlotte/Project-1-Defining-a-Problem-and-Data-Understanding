Key Insights

  • Higher study load is associated with higher stress.
  • Better sleep quality tends to reduce stress levels.
  • Stronger social support slightly buffers stress.
  • Academic performance shows a visible relationship with stress distribution.

Conclusion

This analysis shows that academic workload, sleep, and social factors significantly influence student stress.
Universities could improve student well-being by encouraging balanced study habits, better sleep hygiene, and stronger peer-support networks.
